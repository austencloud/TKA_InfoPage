The Kinetic Alphabet

A choreography toolbox

Motions:

Prospin

A prospin (AKA an isolation) is when a staff moves to an adjacent point while rotating in the same direction that the hands are moving around the center point.

Antispin

An antipin is when a staff moves to an adjacent point while rotating in the opposite direction that the hands are moving around the center point.

Float

In Tier 1, a float is when a staff moves to an adjacent point without rotating at all. In higher tiers, you can add primes (180-degree rotations) to a float to end in the same position. Floats always traverse from one Layer to another.

Dash

A dash is when a staff moves directly across the center point to the point that is on the opposite side of the grid.

Still

A still is when a staff stays in place at its point on the grid for a beat. In higher tiers, a still can be modified with primes (180-degree rotations).
